ABOUT

Ethan is from Lexington County, where he enjoys being involved in his community and spending time with his family, friends, and pets. His Christian faith is an important part of his life and guides the way he approaches his relationships, responsibilities, and service.

Outside of 4‑H, Ethan is active in FFA and serves as the Batesburg‑Leesville FFA Chapter Chaplain. He enjoys reading, writing poetry, listening to music, watching sports, and spending time outdoors through activities like fishing, hiking, and traveling.

Ethan also has a passion for history and enjoys collecting coins from different time periods and places around the world. That same interest in culture and design has led him to study architecture as a hobby, where he enjoys learning how buildings, spaces, and structure shape the way people live and interact.

Looking toward the future, Ethan hopes to pursue a career in business. He has a passion for marketing and design, enjoys creating ideas, and hopes to one day become an entrepreneur.

4-H JOURNEY

2023

Ethan began his 4-H journey by exploring new opportunities and becoming involved in his county's programs.

  • Joined Lexington County 4-H
  • Joined Lexington County Outdoor Explorers Club

2024

Ethan expanded his involvement through projects and competitions, developing his interests in conservation, fishing, and creative expression.

  • 4-H State Fishing Project — 2nd Place, Junior Division
  • 4-H Horse Creative Contest — 1st Place Poetry
  • 4-H WHEP — Junior Participant

2025

Ethan’s involvement grew through leadership roles, state-level competition, and an opportunity to represent SC 4-H on the national level.

  • Served as Lexington County 4-H Teen Council Treasurer
  • Selected as a 4-H State Youth Ambassador
  • 4-H State Fishing Project — 2nd Place, Senior Division
  • 4-H WHEP — State Champion Team Member, Senior Division
  • 4-H WHEP — 5th Place, National Competition
  • 4-H Horse Creative Contest — 1st Place Essay and 2nd Place Poetry
  • Attended 4-H State Congress

2026

Ethan continues serving in leadership roles while building on the experiences and opportunities that 4-H has provided.

  • Continued serving as a 4-H State Youth Ambassador
  • Continued serving as Lexington County 4-H Teen Council Treasurer
  • 4-H Horse Creative Contest — 1st Place Poetry
  • Candidate for Midlands Representative

ETHAN'S MESSAGE

These principles reflect the type of leader I strive to be, and the way I hope to serve as Midlands Representative.

Serving Responsibly

Leadership begins with service. Through my experiences in 4-H, I have learned that being a leader means putting others first, listening, and following through.

As Midlands Representative, I will serve responsibly by representing every county, valuing every member, and making decisions with the best interest of 4-H in mind.

Leading with Consistency

Strong leadership requires commitment and reliability. Through roles like Lexington County 4-H Teen Council Treasurer and State Youth Ambassador, I have learned the importance of showing up and doing the work.

As Midlands Representative, I will lead with consistency by staying involved, keeping my commitments, and being a dependable voice for the Midlands.

Building Strong Connections

The greatest part of 4-H is the people who make it special. I believe strong connections create stronger communities.

As Midlands Representative, I hope to bring members together, encourage collaboration, and help every member feel connected and represented.

Together, we are MAKING MIDLANDS MORE!
